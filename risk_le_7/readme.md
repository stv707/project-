## The Risk Management Mindset

Risk management is not about predicting the future perfectly.

It is about:
- Increasing **known risks**
- Reducing **unknown risks**

In the mobile app project, unknown risks include:
- Undocumented API limitations
- Unclear regulatory requirements
- Hidden dependencies between features

Every conversation, prototype, and technical spike turns unknown risk into known risk.

## Risk Ownership Matters

During a planning meeting, the team identifies a risk:
> â€œThe payment gateway may not support certain local banks.â€

## Detectability: The Silent Risk Multiplier

Some risks are dangerous not because they are severe, but because they are **hard to detect**.

Example:
- A memory leak in the mobile app that only appears after long usage
- A security flaw that is invisible until exploited

Low detectability means:

- Until damage is already done

Smart PMs invest in:
- Monitoring
- Logging
- Early warning signals

## Probability and Impact: A Thinking Tool, Not a Formula

Not all risks deserve equal attention.

Ask yourself:
- How likely is this risk?
- If it happens, how bad is it?

A rare but catastrophic risk (e.g. payment data breach) deserves more attention than a frequent but minor UI bug.

The goal is **focus**, not mathematical perfection.

When facing a risk, a PM has options:

- **Avoid**: Remove a risky feature entirely
- **Mitigate**: Reduce likelihood or impact
- **Transfer**: Use insurance or vendor contracts
- **Accept**: Acknowledge and monitor
- **Exploit**: Actively pursue positive risk

There is no â€œbestâ€ strategy â€” only **appropriate choices**.
